## Leverage, Liquidation, and Risk Controls Worth Knowing

Every Filipino futures beginner asks the same question: "How much leverage should I use?" The honest answer is that the right number is lower than whatever you're currently thinking.

OKX's liquidation engine uses a multi-exchange **mark price** rather than the last traded price, which prevents manipulative wicks from triggering unfair liquidations. When your margin ratio drops below the maintenance threshold, the system triggers partial or full liquidation depending on how far underwater the position is.

At a practical level, here's how leverage maps to liquidation risk on a long position:

| Leverage | Approx. adverse move before liquidation |
| --- | --- |
| 2x | ~50% |
| 5x | ~20% |
| 10x | ~10% |
| 25x | ~4% |
| 50x | ~2% |
| 100x | ~1% |

The built-in **position calculator** on the order entry panel shows your estimated liquidation price and maintenance margin before you submit — use it every single time. Set stop-loss orders on every position. Enable push notifications for margin call warnings. These aren't optional habits; they're the difference between staying in the game and getting wiped out in a single funding cycle.

For risk that extends beyond individual positions, OKX maintains an **insurance fund** funded by a portion of trading fees. In extreme market events where liquidations can't be matched, the insurance fund covers the deficit before auto-deleveraging (ADL) kicks in. ADL is the last-resort mechanism where highly profitable opposing positions get auto-closed to keep the system solvent — rare, but it has happened during violent market moves, which is why setting your own stop-loss is always preferable to relying on the platform's backstops.
